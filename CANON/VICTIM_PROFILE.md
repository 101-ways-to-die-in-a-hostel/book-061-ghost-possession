# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Harriet "Harry" Blackwood
**Nickname/Handle:** @SkepticalHarry
**Age:** 29
**Nationality:** British (London)
**Occupation:** YouTuber/Podcast Host - "Bullshit Busters"

## Archetype
**Primary:** Skeptic Influencer

**Secondary traits:** Former philosophy student, aggressive debater, closet adrenaline junkie who gets high on "winning" arguments

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Hubris

**How it manifests:**
- Constantly films herself "debunking" local spiritual practices
- Mocks hostel guests who believe in ghosts with theatrical eye-rolls
- Challenges spirits to "prove they exist" with performative bravado

**How it kills them:**
Her public challenge to the ghost to possess her - done for content - is accepted. Her refusal to believe what's happening delays any attempt to seek help until the possession is complete.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 5'7", athletic but not muscular, restless energy
- **Hair:** Sharp black bob, always perfectly maintained
- **Distinguishing features:** Nose ring, collection of ironic religious symbol tattoos, perpetual smirk
- **Typical outfit:** Band t-shirts of obscure punk groups, leather jacket even in heat

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Intelligence - becomes intellectual bullying
2. Confidence - becomes inability to admit she might be wrong

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Contempt for believers - leads to underestimating genuine danger
2. Need for content/engagement - leads to increasingly reckless challenges

### Pet Peeves
- People who say "there are things science can't explain"
- Hostel mates who burn sage or carry crystals

### Fears
- Surface fear: Being seen as gullible
- Deeper fear: That her entire worldview might be wrong

## Background

### Origin Story
Raised by devoutly religious parents in a strict evangelical household. Rebellion against childhood indoctrination led to militant atheism. Philosophy degree at Edinburgh gave her the vocabulary to articulate her contempt. Started YouTube channel after debunking a local "psychic" who'd taken money from grieving families.

### Why They Travel
Collecting content. She's on a "World Superstition Tour" - visiting supposedly haunted locations and proving them fraudulent for her 2.3 million subscribers.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Edinburgh hostel in the Old Town was featured on "Most Haunted Hostels" list. She booked it specifically to film a debunking episode.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "empirically," "demonstrably false," "cognitive bias," "logical fallacy"
- Avoids words like: "maybe," "I don't know," "what if"
- Catchphrases: "Show me the evidence," "Extraordinary claims require extraordinary proof"

### Speech Pattern
- Rapid-fire sentences, often interrupts others
- British accent sharpened for emphasis
- Heavy sarcasm, theatrical sighs

### Internal Monologue Style
Constantly composing arguments, imagining how she'll edit this moment for content, scoring points against invisible opponents

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** YouTube, Twitter for engagement bait
- **Follower count:** 2.3 million across platforms
- **Posting style:** Confrontational, reaction videos, "destroyed with facts and logic" content
- **Last post before death:** Video titled "I'm Going to Let a Ghost Possess Me (LOL)" with thumbnail of her making mocking prayer hands

### Online vs Reality
Online she's supremely confident; in reality, she's exhausted from maintaining the persona and terrified of losing relevance

## Relationships

### Family
- Estranged from evangelical parents who pray for her soul
- Sister still speaks to her but relationship is strained

### Friends
- Collaborator friends, not real friends - everyone is potential content
- No one she'd call in a genuine emergency

### Romantic
- Serial dater, commitment issues, views relationships as distractions

### At the Hostel
- Immediately antagonizes guests who share ghost stories
- Befriends a cameraman backpacker who agrees to film her

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Films intro segment about the hostel's "ridiculous ghost legend" with maximum contempt

### Their Last Normal Moment
Laughing with her cameraman about how "pathetic" the seance will be

### Final Thoughts
"It's real. It was all real. I can't move. I can't breathe. I can feel it wearing me like a coat. Mom was right. Oh god, mom was right."

### Last Words
"Get out of me!" (spoken in her own voice briefly breaking through)
